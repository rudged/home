Below are links to genealogies of several branches of the Rudge family.

  • Evesham branch 1587>present; Seisdon, Stafford Co. >Evesham, Worcester Co. England

    - this is the landed Rudge family associated with Evesham referred to in
    Burke's Landed Gentry

  • Gloucester branch 1726>1864; Gloucester >London, England

    - this is a branch of the Rudge family associated with Gloucester cathedral, that
    at least one heraldry book refers to as being awarded a coat of arms, by virtue
    of its unspecified connection with the family centered at Evesham.

  • Cirencester branch 1602>1752; Cirencester, Co. Gloucester >London, England

    - this branch of the Rudge family applied for, but was not awarded a coat of
    arms, because they could not establish their connection to the Evesham Rudge
    family.

  • Smethwick branch 1741>present; Smethwick, Stafford Co. England
    - this is the branch I have been doing research on during 2008, as I believe I have discovered a connection to the North Carolina branch below from which I descend

  • North Carolina branch 1799>present; Southwark (London) England>Monroe, NC
    - this is my branch of the Rudge family; no clear connection yet to the family of
    Evesham, but interesting nevertheless

  • Brazil branch 1792>present; Gloucester England>Brazil
    - this is a branch of the Rudge family that migrated to South America. I am not doing research on this branch and am not updating it

Here's a comprehensive index of individuals mentioned in the above five genealogies (doesn't yet include the Smethwick branch).

Other resources to help you with your research on the Rudge family regarding the above five branches.

  • New Zealand branch1834>present; Wolverhampton, England>New Zealand
    - this is a branch of the Rudge family associated with the Rudge Bicycle company
    that migrated to New Zealand. It is the result of long and hard work by Marie
    Cooper and is located on a site maintained by a genealogy society specializing in
    families that lived in Wolverhampton, England. The site also contains a link to
    information about the Rudge Bike Company.
