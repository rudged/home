Descendants of John De Rugge of Seisdon, Co. Stafford, England

The following is a genealogy of the Rudge family associated with Evesham, England based on Frankford (ed.) (ca. 1910) "Rudge, The Name and The Arms", a three page pamphlet brought to my attention by Scott Rudge on 3 Aug 1998. It is clearly an extract from a much larger unnamed landed gentry published some time after 1828, but before 13 May 1841 as it contains no mention of the present esquire of Evesham 57. Edward Rudge as having remarried. The text claims to be based on "manorial grants cited in Shaw's Staffordshire." I have supplemented it with information taken from Burke's (1952) Landed Gentry (17th edition) pp. 2216-7 which takes up the story with 6. John RUGGE and a few additional sources as indicated.

Arms were recorded at the College of Arms in 1634 for Edward Rudge of London, salter, son of Thomas Rudge of Evesham in Worcestershire, "whose Auncestors came formerlie out of Cheshire" at the College of Arms in London in 1634. The shield was blazoned, Quarterly sable and gules, overall a cross engrailed argent. Crest- issuing out of a mural coronet or, two arms 'erected' habited gules, cuffs and hands proper, the hands holding an escutcheon argent. The wording implies that the family had been using this design already, and that it was not a new Grant of Arms- the word 'grant' is not actually used - the text instead says 'exemplifie, confirme, ratefie and allow.' In 1790, Edward Rudge purchased a moiety of the estate of Seymour Biscoe, esq. at Evesham, and in 1792, a considerable part of that moiety of the lordship of Braybrooke, in Northamptonshire, which belonged to John Hopkins, esq. on the division of the lordship between him and John Rudge, esq. in 1778, under the enclosure act of parliament; the other portion, together with the manorial rights, having been, with the Castle estate, previously sold to Lord Braybrooke, by whom it was disposed of by auction, in July, 1812. Mr. Rudge also purchased various closes of land, of considerable extent, at Evesham, and built the Abbey Manor House in 1816.

On 28 Jul 1791 Edward first married Anne NOUAILLE, the only daughter of Peter NOUAILLE esq. of Great Ness House co. Kent, J.P. & Anne WOODGATE, at St. George's, Hanover-square. She was born on 29 Oct 1761. Anne died on 1 Sep 1836.
